Cookie Policy

Effective: 2026-05-23 · Applies to: dwightaspencer.com

tl;dr

This site sets no first-party cookies. No consent banner is required because I am not setting tracking or advertising cookies. One third-party security cookie may be set by Cloudflare; it does not track you across sites.

What is a cookie

A cookie is a small text file stored in your browser by a website. Cookies are used for purposes ranging from keeping you logged in to tracking your behaviour across the web for advertising. This site uses neither login sessions nor advertising networks.

Cookies set by this site

Name Source Purpose Duration Tracking?
__cf_bm Cloudflare (CDN/DDoS protection) Bot mitigation — distinguishes human visitors from automated traffic 30 minutes No — scoped to session; not used for cross-site tracking

No other cookies are set by this site or its code. The __cf_bm cookie is a technical necessity of Cloudflare's security layer, not a choice made by this site's author, and does not identify you individually or persist beyond your session.

Third-party requests that may set cookies

Some pages load resources from external domains. These third parties operate under their own cookie policies:

  • Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com) — Google states it does not use font requests to build advertising profiles or set persistent cookies, but your IP is logged. See Google Fonts Privacy FAQ.
  • Stack Exchange (stackexchange.com) — the flair badge image on the homepage is fetched from Stack Exchange servers. Stack Exchange may set session cookies if you are logged into their network. See Stack Exchange Cookie Policy.

Future analytics

If Plausible Analytics is enabled in future, it operates without cookies, without fingerprinting, and without any cross-site tracking. This policy will be updated if and when that changes.

Managing cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Blocking the __cf_bm cookie may cause some pages to load more slowly or trigger additional security checks, but the site will remain fully functional.
