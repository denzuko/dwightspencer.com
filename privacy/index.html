Privacy Policy

Effective: 2026-05-23 · Applies to: dwightaspencer.com

tl;dr

This site does not track you. No advertising. No behavioral profiling. No personal data sold or shared. Contact is via LinkedIn or Keybase — not email.

1. Who is responsible

Dwight Spencer, operating as a private individual at dwightaspencer.com. Contact: Keybase for encrypted correspondence, LinkedIn for general inbound. Mailing address available on request for legal correspondence only (NYS Address Confidentiality Program participant).

2. What data is collected

2.1 Server and CDN logs

This site is served via Cloudflare. Cloudflare may log IP addresses, request paths, timestamps, user-agent strings, and referrer headers as part of normal CDN and DDoS-protection operation. These logs are held by Cloudflare under their own privacy policy. I receive only aggregate traffic summaries.

No server-side analytics scripts are deployed by me. Cloudflare provides aggregate analytics as a byproduct of CDN operation; a privacy-respecting Plausible Analytics integration is planned as an opt-in A/B test when it goes live this policy will be updated.

2.2 Contact

There is no contact form on this site. Inbound contact is via LinkedIn or Keybase. I do not add you to any mailing list, share your information, or retain correspondence beyond operational necessity.

2.3 Cookies

I set no first-party cookies. See the Cookie Policy for the full inventory including the Cloudflare __cf_bm security cookie.

2.4 Third-party resources

Pages on this site load the following external resources:

  • Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com) — Bricolage Grotesque typeface. Your IP is logged by Google when the font is fetched. See the Google Fonts Privacy FAQ. Self-hosting is on the roadmap.
  • Stack Exchange (stackexchange.com) — profile flair badge image on the homepage. Stack Exchange may set session cookies if you are logged into their network.
  • Avataaars (avataaars.io) — avatar illustration. Generated server-side, no cookies.

No advertising networks, social media tracking pixels, or behavioral analytics are intentionally loaded.

2.5 Analytics roadmap

Plausible Analytics — cookieless, no fingerprinting, GDPR-compliant by design — is planned as an A/B test against Cloudflare aggregate data. If and when enabled, this policy and the Cookie Policy will be updated and a notice added to the site.

3. Legal bases (GDPR)

For EEA visitors:

  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) — CDN logging for security and availability.
  • Contract / pre-contractual (Art. 6(1)(b)) — processing inbound contact to respond to your inquiry.

4. Your rights

Under GDPR (EEA), NY SHIELD Act, and CCPA (California) you have rights including access, correction, erasure, restriction, objection, and portability. Contact via Keybase to exercise any right. CDN logs held by Cloudflare must be addressed to Cloudflare directly.

5. AI training and data mining

All content is explicitly opted out of AI training, TDM, and automated extraction. See the Data Usage Policy for the full statement and machine-readable references.

6. Children

This site is not directed at children under 13 (US) or 16 (EU).

7. Changes

Material changes will be noted in recent writing and reflected in the effective date above. The Git commit history for this file is the authoritative change log.
